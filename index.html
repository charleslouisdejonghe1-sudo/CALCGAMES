Questions

FAQ

Common questions about calculator game installation.

Will this break my calculator? ▼
No! Games are just programs stored in your calculator's memory. You can delete them any time by pressing MEM → Manage Memory. Your calculator stays exactly the same — just with games on it.
What if I get an "Error: Invalid" message? ▼
This happens if you have a newer OS on your CE calculator. The OS update disabled running assembly games. The fix is a quick jailbreak — included free in the Full Load package. It's totally reversible.
Will the games disappear after a battery change? ▼
RAM memory can clear if the battery dies completely. However, I archive the games to Flash memory, which survives battery changes. If they do get wiped, use your Update Pass for a cheap reinstall!
Do I need to bring anything besides my calculator? ▼
Just bring your TI-84 and its USB charging cable (the one that comes in the box). That's it — I have everything else needed for the installation.
Will this mess up my school work / apps on the calculator? ▼
Not at all. Games are stored as programs and don't touch your saved calculations, notes, or Texas Instruments apps. Everything stays separate and clean.
